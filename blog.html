2. Amboseli national park

December,04 2017

A photograph of African bush elephant and a view of mount kilimajaro the highest mountain in Africa.

Amboseli national park is located in Kajiado county 250km from nairobi. It has coverage area of 39,206 hectares that that spreads across the Kenya-Tanzania border. The park is famous for being the best place in the world to get close to elephants. Other atractions include massai community villages and a clear view of mount kilimajaro. Amboseli National Park is hosts many species, which include African bush elephants, Cape buffaloes, impala, East African lions, cheetahs, hyenas, Masai giraffes, plains zebras and blue wildebeest, birds among other African animals. There are swamps, dried lake and semi-arid vegetation.

Amboseli national park started as a national reserve in 1906 and later in 1974 it was renamed Amboseli national park. It is governed by Kenya wildlife service and the Maasai community.

Historical and Archaeological sites

1. Olorgesailie

December,05 2017

A photograph taken from olorgesailie Olorgesailie Archaeological site.

Olorgesailie is located 64 km from Nairobi along the road heading to Lake Magadi. Olorgesailie is known for the large number of aechulean hand axes and other human tools used during homo erectus era. Fossils of various animals have also been found likely to have been butchered with the use of the hand axes. 970,000 years old human fossils that belong to homo erectus were found in this site in 2003 by a team led by Potts.

Olorgesailie governed by National Museums of Kenya.

2. Ruins of Gedi.

December,05 2017

Walls at Gedi.

The ruins of Gedi are a historical and archaeological site located in in the Kilifi District, Kenya near the coast of eastern Kenya.The site of Gedi comprises of walled town with buildings, which include mosques, palace, and numerous houses, made from stone and settlement area which contain earth and thatch houses. The pillar tombs at Gedi, consist of masonry based structures topped with a pillar. Cultural materials found in this site include cowrie shells (currency), beads and numerous types and styles of ceramics.

3.Fort Jesus.

December,05 2017

Fort Jesus from outside.

Fort Jesus is a fort located at Monbasa Island. It was built between 1593 and 1596 by portuguese to guard the port of Mombasa. The architecture of the fort assembles a figure of a person lying on their back. The height of the walls is 18 meters high. Some of the historical structures in the fort include Oman House, where Sultan lived and from where he governed the East African coast. There are open water cistern for harvesting rain water made by portuguese, and a deep well sunk by the Arabs.

4.Hyrax Hill.

December,05 2017

Fort Jesus from outside.

This prehistoric site is located in Nakuru. It is known for the found fossils of Iron Age 3,000 years ago. The hill was named after hyraxes which are found living in cracks within rocks found in this area. First discovered by a group led by L.S.B. Leakey.

1.Menengai Crater

December,05 2017

A photograph of menegai crater.

It is located in the great rift valley in Nakuru,kenya. It is the largest volcano caldera in Kenya and the second largest volcano caldera in Africa. The volcano was formed 200,000 years ago. The caldera was later formed about 8000 years ago covering 12 x 8 km .The caldera floor is covered with post caldera lava flows. Today, volcanic activity continues and is used to generate geothermal power.
